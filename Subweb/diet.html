Diet

We generally refer to “diet” as synonymous with a partial or almost total deprivation of food. However, the correct meaning of Diet is the set of
foods and dishes that are consumed each day, and constitutes the unit of nutrition.
Unlike the correct diet, correct nutrition refers to eating habits that meet the specific needs at different stages of life,
promotes adequate growth and development in boys and girls, and in adults allows maintaining or achieving the expected weight for height and prevents
the development of diseases.

Guide to achieving good nutrition on a pureed food diet or an easy-to-chew soft food diet

Eat foods that contain all the nutrients the body needs to stay healthy. The body needs:

  • Proteins.
  • Carbohydrates.
  • Fats.
  • Fiber.
  • Vitamins and minerals.

Make sure you drink enough fluids. Most people should drink 8 (8-ounce) glasses of water a day. Ask your doctor or registered
dietitian nutritionist if drinking more or less fluid is right for you.

Protein

Protein helps the body to form tissues and heal after surgery. Foods rich in protein include:

  • The soft meat.
  • Fish.
  • Poultry (chicken, turkey).
  • Milk, whether cow's milk or powder.
  • Cheese and cottage cheese.
  • Yogurt, particularly Greek.
  • The eggs and their whites.
  • Beans and mashed beans.
  • Creamy nut butter, like peanut butter.
  • Soy products, such as soy milk, tofu, and edamame (soybeans).
  • Protein powders.
  • Liquid nutritional supplements, such as Ensure®, Ensure Plus®, Boost® or Boost Plus®.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are starches and sugars. Most of the carbohydrates in your diet should come from the following foods:

  • Starchy vegetables, such as potatoes, peas, and squash.
  • Whole grains, such as oats and brown rice.
  • Cereals.
  • Breads.
  • Pasta.

Grease

Grease is the source with the highest concentration of calories. For example, 1 teaspoon of oil (grease) has 45 calories, while
1 teaspoon of sugar (carbohydrate) contains 20. Including some grease your diet is healthy and necessary.
Fats are found in the following foods:

  • Soft meats.
  • Dairy.
  • Coconut and milk or canned coconut cream.
  • Creamy nut butter, like peanut butter.
  • Seeds.
  • Vegetable oils.
  • Avocados.
  • Olives.
  • Fried and sautéed foods.
  • Baked products.

Some fats are healthier than others. Unsaturated fats are healthier than saturated fats.

Unsaturated fats are found in:

  • The olive oil.
  • Canola oil.
  • Peanut oil.
  • Fish oil.
  • The avocado.

Saturated fats are found in:

  • Soft meats.
  • Non-skim dairy products, such as whole milk, cheeses, full-fat cream, and cream cheese.
  • Butter.
  • Coconut and products derived from it, such as coconut oil.
  • Palm oil.
